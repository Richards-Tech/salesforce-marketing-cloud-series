# 📅 Day 29 – SFMC Learning Series | Learn With Me

## 📩 Email Deliverability Advanced

👉 This is where beginners stop… and experts stand out.  
If your emails are landing in spam, it’s not bad luck —  
It’s how mailbox providers evaluate you.  

---

### 🔥 1. IP Warming Strategy
You can’t send 100K emails on Day 1.  

👉 Gradually increase volume  
👉 Start with highly engaged users  
👉 Maintain consistent sending patterns  

💡 ISPs track: sudden spikes = suspicious behavior  

---

### 🎯 2. Engagement-Based Sending
Not all subscribers are equal.  

👉 Send more to:  
- Recent openers  
- Click-active users  

👉 Reduce or pause:  
- Inactive users  
- No-engagement segments  

💡 Higher engagement = better inbox placement  

---

### ⚠️ 3. Spam Trap Avoidance
Spam traps = hidden landmines 🚫  

**Types:**  
- Pristine traps (never opted-in)  
- Recycled traps (old inactive emails)  

👉 Avoid by:  
- No purchased lists  
- Regular list cleaning  
- Double opt-in strategy  

---

### 📊 4. Sender Reputation
Your **IP + Domain = Your identity**  

ISPs track:  
- Bounce rate  
- Spam complaints  
- Engagement rate  

💡 Bad reputation = straight to spam (or blocked)  

---

### 🔐 5. Domain Authentication (Must-Have)
✔ SPF – Verifies sending server  
✔ DKIM – Ensures message integrity  
✔ DMARC – Policy + reporting layer  

👉 Without these → You’re not trusted  

---

### 🧠 How ISPs Judge Your Emails
Mailbox providers like Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo Mail use advanced filtering.  

They evaluate:  
✔ Sender reputation  
✔ User engagement (opens, clicks, replies)  
✔ Complaint signals (“Mark as spam”)  
✔ Sending consistency  
✔ Authentication validity  

💡 **Final verdict:**  
👉 “Do users want this email?”  
- If YES → Inbox 📥  
- If NO → Spam 🚫  

---

### 🚀 Pro Insight (What Experts Do)
👉 Segment before sending  
👉 Warm IPs like a strategy, not a task  
👉 Prioritize engagement over volume  
👉 Monitor reputation continuously
